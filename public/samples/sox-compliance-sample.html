1.Executive Summary

1.1Assessment Overview

This Internal Controls over Financial Reporting (ICFR) documentation has been prepared in compliance with Section 404 of the Sarbanes-Oxley Act of 2002. The assessment evaluates the effectiveness of internal controls designed to provide reasonable assurance regarding the reliability of financial reporting and preparation of financial statements for external purposes.

SOX Section 404(a): Management shall establish and maintain an adequate internal control structure and procedures for financial reporting and include in the company's annual report an assessment of the effectiveness of the internal control structure and procedures for financial reporting.

1.2Management Assessment Conclusion

Based on our evaluation of internal controls over financial reporting as of December 31, 2024, management has concluded that our internal controls are effective and provide reasonable assurance that:

  • Financial reporting is reliable and accurate
  • Transactions are properly authorized and recorded
  • Assets are adequately safeguarded
  • Applicable laws and regulations are followed

2.Internal Control Framework

2.1COSO Framework Implementation

Our internal control system is based on the Committee of Sponsoring Organizations of the Treadway Commission (COSO) 2013 framework. The framework consists of five integrated components:

COSO Framework Components

2.2Significant Accounts and Processes

The following accounts and processes have been identified as significant to our financial reporting:

  1. Revenue Recognition: Order-to-cash process, contract management, and revenue cutoff procedures
  2. Accounts Receivable: Customer credit evaluation, collection procedures, and allowance estimation
  3. Inventory Management: Physical inventory controls, costing methodologies, and obsolescence reserves
  4. Fixed Assets: Capital expenditure approval, depreciation calculations, and impairment testing
  5. Accounts Payable: Vendor management, three-way matching, and accrual processes
  6. Payroll and Benefits: Time reporting, approval hierarchies, and benefits administration
  7. Financial Close: Period-end procedures, journal entries, and management review processes

Control Objective

Ensure that revenue is recognized in accordance with applicable accounting standards (ASC 606) and that all revenue transactions are properly authorized, recorded, and reported in the correct accounting period.

3.2Financial Close and Reporting Controls

Month-End Close Process Controls

  1. Account Reconciliations: All balance sheet accounts reconciled and reviewed monthly
  2. Journal Entry Review: All manual journal entries require dual approval
  3. Management Review: Financial statements reviewed by CFO before issuance
  4. Variance Analysis: Significant variances investigated and documented
  5. Supporting Documentation: All adjustments supported by appropriate documentation
