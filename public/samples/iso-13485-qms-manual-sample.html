1.Introduction and Scope

1.1Purpose

This Quality Management System (QMS) Manual describes the quality management system of [Company Name] and demonstrates our commitment to meeting ISO 13485:2016 requirements for medical devices. This manual serves as the foundational document for our quality management system and provides the framework for all quality-related activities.

ISO 13485:2016 Clause 4.2.2: The organization shall establish and maintain a quality manual that includes the scope of the quality management system, documented procedures established for the quality management system, and a description of the interaction between the processes of the quality management system.

1.2Scope of Application

This QMS applies to all activities related to the design, development, production, installation, and servicing of medical devices at [Company Name]. The scope includes:

  • Class I and Class II medical devices
  • Software as Medical Device (SaMD)
  • Manufacturing processes and controls
  • Post-market surveillance activities
  • Regulatory compliance management

2.Quality Policy and Objectives

Quality Policy: [Company Name] is committed to designing, manufacturing, and distributing medical devices that consistently meet customer requirements and applicable regulatory standards. 3.Organizational Structure and Responsibilities

3.1Management Responsibility

Top management demonstrates leadership and commitment with respect to the quality management system by:

Management Responsibilities

  1. Establishing and maintaining the quality policy and quality objectives
  2. Ensuring customer and regulatory requirements are determined and met
  3. Conducting management reviews at planned intervals
  4. Ensuring the availability of necessary resources
  5. Promoting awareness of regulatory requirements throughout the organization

3.2Quality Management Representative

Appointment: The Quality Manager serves as the management representative with responsibility and authority for ensuring that processes needed for the quality management system are established, implemented, and maintained.

4.Document Control and Records Management

4.1Document Control Procedure

All quality system documents are controlled in accordance with procedure DOC-CTRL-001. The document control system ensures:

Document Control Process

  1. Documents are approved for adequacy prior to issue
  2. Documents are reviewed, updated, and re-approved as necessary
  3. Changes and current revision status are identified
  4. Relevant versions are available at points of use
  5. Documents are legible and readily identifiable
  6. External documents are identified and controlled
  7. Obsolete documents are prevented from unintended use

4.2Records Management

Quality records provide evidence of conformity to requirements and effective operation of the quality management system. Records are maintained according to the Record Control Matrix (RCM-001).

5.Risk Management

5.1Risk Management Process

Risk management activities are conducted in accordance with ISO 14971:2019 and are integrated throughout the product lifecycle. The risk management process includes:

Risk Management Activities

  1. Risk analysis and evaluation during design and development
  2. Risk control measure implementation and verification
  3. Residual risk evaluation and acceptability assessment
  4. Risk management file maintenance and updates
  5. Post-market surveillance data integration
ISO 13485:2016 Clause 7.1: The organization shall establish documented procedures for risk management in design and development. Risk management activities shall be documented in the design and development files.
