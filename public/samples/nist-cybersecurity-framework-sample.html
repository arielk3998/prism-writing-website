1. Framework Overview

The NIST Cybersecurity Framework provides a policy framework of computer security guidance for how private sector organizations can assess and improve their ability to prevent, detect, and respond to cyber attacks.

1.1 Framework Structure

The Framework consists of six core functions that provide a high-level view of cybersecurity risk management:

GV
GOVERN
Organizational cybersecurity risk management strategy
ID
IDENTIFY
Asset and risk management understanding
PR
PROTECT
Safeguards and protective measures
DE
DETECT
Monitoring and detection capabilities
RS
RESPOND
Incident response and mitigation
RC
RECOVER
Recovery planning and resilience

2. Implementation Tiers

2.1 Current State Assessment

Implementation Tiers describe the degree to which cybersecurity risk management practices exhibit characteristics defined in the Framework:

Tier 1: Partial Tier 2: Risk Informed Tier 3: Repeatable Tier 4: Adaptive

GOVERN (GV) Function

The organization's cybersecurity risk management strategy, expectations, and policy are established, communicated, and monitored.

Category Subcategory Current State Implementation Status
GV.OC: Organizational Context GV.OC-01: Organizational mission is understood Tier 3 Complete
GV.RM: Risk Management Strategy GV.RM-01: Risk management objectives are established Tier 2 In Progress
GV.RR: Roles and Responsibilities GV.RR-01: Organizational leadership is responsible for cybersecurity risk Tier 3 Complete
GV.PO: Policy GV.PO-01: Policy for managing cybersecurity risks is established Tier 2 In Progress

IDENTIFY (ID) Function

The organization's current cybersecurity posture is understood by identifying and managing cybersecurity risks to systems, people, assets, data, and capabilities.

3. Gap Analysis and Roadmap

3.1 Priority Implementation Areas

Based on risk assessment and current maturity levels, the following areas require immediate attention:

  1. Asset Inventory and Classification (ID.AM)
    • Complete hardware and software asset discovery
    • Implement data classification framework
    • Establish asset ownership and accountability
  2. Access Control Enhancement (PR.AC)
    • Deploy multi-factor authentication
    • Implement privileged access management
    • Regular access reviews and certification
  3. Detection and Response (DE.CM, RS.RP)
    • 24/7 security monitoring implementation
    • Incident response plan testing
    • Threat intelligence integration
